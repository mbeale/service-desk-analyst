## Competitor Insights

### Atlassian (Jira Service Management)
- **Fact:** JSM includes OpsGenie for alert management.
- **Assumption:** Organizations prioritizing DevOps and Jira are less likely to convert to SWSD.

### EasyVista
- **Fact:** Strong market presence in Europe.
- **Fact:** North American sales team consists largely of former Cherwell employees.
- **Assumption:** UI is outdated compared to modern ITSM tools.

### Freshworks
- **Fact:** Restricts critical features like AI, CMDB, and Audits to higher tiers or add-ons.
- **Fact:** Offers 24/5 phone support (vs. SWSD 24/7).
- **Inference:** UI demos well but may hide cumbersome workflows in practice.

### HaloITSM
- **Fact:** Lacks native project management and complex shift management features.
- **Assumption:** Configuration is clunky compared to SWSD.

### Ivanti
- **Fact:** Acquired Cherwell, forcing a migration path for legacy customers.
- **Inference:** Focuses on complex enterprise needs, often resulting in higher implementation friction.

### ManageEngine
- **Fact:** Heavily discounts Year 1 pricing, often leading to significant cost increases at renewal.
- **Assumption:** Relies on a loss-leader strategy for initial acquisition.

### NinjaOne
- **Fact:** Primarily an RMM tool; lacks deep ITSM functionality like Change Management.

### ServiceNow
- **Fact:** Requires specialized consultants or developers for configuration.
- **Inference:** Often "overkill" for mid-market organizations due to cost and complexity.

### SymphonyAI
- **Fact:** Differentiates via "Copilot" AI automation features.

### SysAid
- **Fact:** Charges extra for customizations and advanced features hidden from base pricing.
